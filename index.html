    About Us

    When I was a boy I barely had any knowledge of computers. What I find funny is that during this preriod of ignorance I was the unknowing owner of a full computer system. Not knowing what I had, and my parents being as ignorant as I was concerning the function of the cumbersome machine, I gave it away one component after the other till all of it was gone.
    I was exposed to computers for the first time sometime during my primary school education. My Computers teacher, Mrs Sithole, asked someone from the class to change the date on one of the computers in the lab during a lesson. Without any prior contact with computers, I boldly raised my hand to attempt the given task. She picked me and I walked boldly to the front of the class. With the click of a mouse, I made my first digitall leap and greeted Microsoft Windows 95 with a trembling smile. I managed to change the date which, to a computer-illiterate young me, was almost miraculous. My heart was pounding with pride as I regained my seat, basking in the admiration of my classmates.
